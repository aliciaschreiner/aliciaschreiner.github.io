Fog is on the Way!

By: Weatherday Editor


Spring weather is up and down as usual and there is some fog rolling into Southeastern Idaho. Be careful when you are out on the roads as visibilty will be low.

Have you ever wondered what fog is and how it forms? Fog occurs when tiny droplets of water pool up together when the air has cooled to the dewpoint. When this happens it can no longer contain the large amount of water vapor. Fog is basically a cloud that stays low to the ground. When the air at the ground gets even colder, fog can turn into ice. This doesn't happen too often in Idaho but usually up North in parts of Canada and Alaska.

If you are planning a trip in the next day or two, be prepared to see lot's of fog on the road. Stay safe out there!
